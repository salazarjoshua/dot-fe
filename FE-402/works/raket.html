Raket: Commission an Artist — a UI/UX Case Study

A mobile app where Filipino artists can offer their services and easily connect with clients.

Introduction

There are artists-for-hire communities in apps such as Facebook, Twitter, and Reddit. They follow a system where artists and clients use tags when posting. #ForHire is used by artists to show their works and general idea of their pricing, whereas, #LookingFor is used by clients with specific requests. Client's posts includes details and/or example of what they're looking for.

Recently, I decided to look for a Filipino artist online. I have browsed through several apps, however, the apps mentioned are not easy to use as they do not have the capability to filter search based on art styles and/or by price range.

THE PROBLEM

Despite having artists-for-hire communities in several apps that help connecting artists and clients, the platform is not really designed for them making it challenging to use.

USERS & AUDIENCE

The target users of Raket are local artists and people who are looking to make a custom art. These people are heavy users of Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.

WHAT I DID

  • Research
  • UX Design
  • UI Design

COMPETITOR RESEARCH

Fiverr is the world's largest freelance service marketplace available, however, I found out that the majority of local artists utilize applications such as Facebook, Instagram and Twitter to promote their artworks and connect with clients. They also use popular local credit cards, G-Cash, and Paymaya as payment methods, and PayPal to accommodate international clients.

I analyzed the different qualities of the above-mentioned apps and identified the features that I can improve, implement, and avoid.

SOME TERMS USED

Think of this as a marketplace like Amazon where there are sellers, buyers, and items.

  • Artists - They are the sellers
  • Clients - They are the buyers
  • Raket(s) - The name of the app. It is also the service (item) offered by the artists (sellers)
  • Local - Filipino or Philippines

Note: I am not affiliated with Raket.ph by Fullstack HQ. I already thought of the name and the idea of what the app would be before seeing their website.

Familiar Apps

Homepage

Since local artists are heavy users of social media, I want the users to think that they are on another social media app.

I purposely made the thumbnail large so users can better appreciate the artwork.

Commission Details

Offered packages are different from artist to artist. Some may offer sketches to full colored illustrations. Some may offer a bust to a full body. But one thing I noticed is packages always have different prices. With this in mind, I labeled the packages by price instead of names such as Basic, Standard and Premium. I believe this is also more convenient to users since this is a marketplace afterall and users are likely to have a budget.

Payment Method

The majority of local artists use local credit/debit cards and e-wallets such as GCash, PayMaya, and Paypal. I included all of this to payment method to accomplish financial transactions faster.

Filter your search results

Searching in apps like Facebook and Twitter is challenging as users have to search the exact phrase or word.

I believe that each person has their own preference in art styles. Filtering search based on art styles, inclusions, and price is made easy.

Anything specific in mind? Post a request.

As mentioned above, there is a system used in artists-for-hire communities. I enhanced the said system separating the #ForHire (Rakets) and #LookingFor (Hiring Artists) sections in order to reduce clutter and improve navigation.

Furthermore, I added a feature where artists can send offers to clients inside the conversation. This will make the order customizable to accommodate specific requests from clients.

Building Trust

Credibility is an essential factor in a marketplace. To help artists make the potential clients comfortable about moving forward with them, I added a Portfolio tab so artists can showcase their previous works. In addition, a feedback section will allow them to gain more trust.

Moving Forward

If I had more time and resources, I would:

  1. Conduct user research and not based them on my assumptions.
  2. Create a prototype and conduct user tests.
  3. Develop the mobile app.
